Treatments

This is what we do specifically, conditions treated, and how we treat them. Would expanding and contracting boxes be a good fit here? How does Cory perform each of these? What tools does he use, and how?

Decompression Therapy: According to my research it's stretching out the spine. It's for pain relief, especially when there's spinal disc issues such as bulging, degeneration, or herniation. Also good for most lower back pains and sciatica nerve pain.

Flexion-Distraction: Slow adjustment to back, also an option for bulging, degenerating, or herniated discs. Same schtick as Decompression Therapy.

Electro Stim Therapy: Shock the patient till they stop hurting basically. Useful in PT as well, for contracting weak/poor functioning muscles. For pain, it should relax muscles, for PT it's most likely going to contract muscles.

Ultrasound: Use vibration to massage muscles. Ya can't hear it, since it's out of our hearing range.

Shockwave therapy: Like ultrasound but bigger and meatier. Instead of a bunch of little waves, it's one big hit, possibly a few times per treatment. Kind of like just shooting someone with a reball. Same function as ultrasound, just different method to achieve the same results.

Percussion Therapy: the goldilocks therapy between ultrasound and shockwave therapy; is more intense per hit than ultrasound, but less frequent waves. It's also not as intense as shockwave's hits, but is more frequent. It's like a deep tissue massage from a masseuse. Great at easing out muscle tension, like a massage. tl;dr a fancy name for a mechanized massage.

Heat therapy: warm things warm the affected area to treat pain. Great for arthritis, stiff muscles, and deep skin tissue injuries

Cryotherapy: Typically a pain relief or inflammation reducer. Bring the affected area to near freezing temps.
